Bike Sales Analysis
  • Projects
  • Project Details

Purpose

Analyze customer and demographic data to identify the main factors influencing bike purchase decisions.

The project translates raw customer records into actionable insights that can support marketing and sales strategy.

Tools

Excel | Pivot Tables | Pivot Charts | Slicers | Dashboard Design

Summary

Built an interactive Excel dashboard to analyze how income, age, commute distance, occupation, and region affect bike purchases.

The dashboard enables dynamic filtering and segmentation, helping stakeholders explore customer behavior and identify high-potential target groups.

Process

  • Cleaned and structured customer data to prepare it for analysis and dashboard reporting.
  • Used pivot tables and charts to explore bike purchases across demographic and economic variables.
  • Analyzed purchase patterns by income, age group, and commute distance to identify key drivers of demand.
  • Built an interactive dashboard with slicers to enable segmentation by marital status, region, occupation, and gender.
  • Translated the findings into business recommendations for customer targeting and campaign focus.

Key Insights

  • Income is a strong purchase driver, with bike buyers showing noticeably higher average income levels.
  • The 30–39 age group represents the strongest buying segment, contributing the highest share of purchases.
  • Customers with a 0–1 mile commute show the highest purchase probability, while demand declines with longer commute distance.
  • The Married + North America + Professional segment shows the highest income levels, but higher income alone does not always guarantee higher purchase rates.

Outcome

Created an interactive Excel dashboard that helps identify target customer segments and supports more focused marketing decisions based on income, age, commute behavior, and demographic patterns.
