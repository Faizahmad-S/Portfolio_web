Retail Sales Analytics
  • Projects
  • Project Details

Purpose

Transform fragmented retail transaction data into a structured analytics pipeline that enables better business decisions around customer value, product performance, and revenue trends.

Tools

SQL | PostgreSQL | VS Code | Job Market Analytics

Summary

Built an end-to-end SQL analytics project that cleans raw retail data, performs exploratory analysis, and generates analytical views used by Power BI dashboards.

The system converts messy CSV transaction data into decision-ready business intelligence covering customer behavior, product performance, and sales growth trends.

Process

  • Cleaned and standardized raw retail CSV data, converting integer dates to SQL DATE format and resolving missing demographic fields using COALESCE().
  • Performed exploratory data analysis to identify business boundaries and calculate core KPIs such as Total Sales, Total Orders, and Active Customers.
  • Applied SQL window functions to analyze time trends and calculate moving averages for long-term pricing patterns.
  • Implemented Year-over-Year product analysis using LAG() to identify growth and decline trends across product categories.
  • Built customer segmentation logic using CTEs and CASE statements to classify customers into VIP, Regular, and New tiers.

Key Insights

  • 85.4% of customers fall into the New category, revealing strong potential for retention strategies.
  • Bike products generate the majority of revenue compared to accessories and clothing.
  • Customers aged 50+ produce the highest order volume across the dataset.
  • Several bike models show consistent YoY growth, while others display steady decline patterns.

Outcome

Created a SQL-driven reporting layer that powers Customer and Product performance dashboards in Power BI, enabling stakeholders to monitor revenue trends, segmentation patterns, and product profitability through a centralized analytics framework.
