Purpose

Analyze the data analyst job market to identify high-paying roles, in-demand skills, and the best skill combinations for career growth.

The project also demonstrates how SQL can be used to extract strategic insights from real-world job posting data.

Tools

SQL | PostgreSQL | VS Code | Job Market Analytics

Summary

Built a SQL analysis project using job posting data to explore salary trends, skill demand, and the most valuable skills for data analyst roles.

The analysis focused on five business questions: top-paying jobs, required skills, most in-demand skills, highest-paying skills, and the optimal skill mix based on both demand and salary.

Process

  • Queried job posting data to identify the top-paying Data Analyst roles, focusing on remote jobs and London-based opportunities with available salary data.
  • Joined job postings with skills tables to determine which tools and technologies appear most often in high-paying analyst roles.
  • Calculated the most in-demand skills across all Data Analyst postings using grouped skill frequency analysis.
  • Computed average salary by skill to identify technical abilities associated with stronger compensation.
  • Combined demand count and salary averages to identify the most optimal skills to learn for long-term career return.

Key Insights

  • SQL is the most consistently in-demand skill, appearing in 180,369 job postings.
  • The strongest demand-based skill set for analyst roles includes SQL, Excel, Python, Tableau, and Power BI.
  • Niche tools such as FastAPI, PyTorch, Airflow, and PostgreSQL show higher salary potential, though they appear in fewer postings.
  • The best overall skill combination based on both demand and salary includes SQL, Tableau, Python, Excel, and Power BI.

Outcome

Created a SQL-driven job market analysis framework that translates job posting data into clear career guidance, helping identify which analyst skills offer the best balance of market demand and earning potential.
